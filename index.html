Canovavirus Outbreak in China:
Weekly Geographical Spread and Critical Statistics

Team Members: Xumin Zhu,Jiamin Li,Jiawei Xia, Xiaohua Shi
Data update: 2/21/2020
Data Souce: https://github.com/CSSEGISandData/COVID-19.git
We are designing two correlated graph to visualize the breakthrough and spread of the conara virus throughout all provinces in China by week.
We've chosen two vis to analyze our data -
  1. A map-based heapmap to show how the disease spread geographically in China with time.
  2. A radical stack bar chart to overview how the exact number of confirmed and death cases are in all provinces.
Encoding Menthods
The reason why we decided to use these two chart types is that the geographical info showing how the disease is spread is the top important attribute to be encoded in this case. Apart from that, we also use color to encode the number of confirmed and death cases, to show the severe situation of virus and its impact on each area. We have once considered to use pyramids or normal ranked bar chart to show the case numbers for the right part of the data vis. However, we finally choose the radical one sincewe want to have a metaphor that all provinces actually connnect wit each other and are part of the destiny of China together.
Interactivity Techniques
We mainly use Hover and Button switch as interactivity in our data vis, trying to eliminate any unnecessary visual distractions.

Our Development Process and Discovered Insights

Develop Process

We geenrally started from choosing a data anlaysis and vis topic, finging suitable datasets, designing the vis, confirming details to cleaning and wrangling data and coding out the design. There are more specific detailed develop process from design to develop


  • We firstly agreed on the most important thing (story) to show through our vis - how the disease has spread and how it has impacted on each area out of our empathy.
  • Then playing with the dataset, we decided the encoding channels: Color to show the impact by representing the scale of case numbers; Geographical location to show its spread route.
  • While there are some methods of map vis to show piecharts or barcharts on it, we decided to make our design simple, clear and self-expressive without requiring much user thinking. Thus, we decied to use two seperate but complementary charts visualizing the geographical spread and numbercal increase of the disease.
  • We've spent a long time translating design into code, espceially the button switch part that listens to the hover interaction and update the whole vis. Thus, we stuck here for a while.
